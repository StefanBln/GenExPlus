# Code of Conduct

GenExPlus is a small, open project. We want it to stay a place where people can ask questions, share ideas, and contribute without worry. This document sets out what we expect — and what we do not tolerate.

## The short version

**Be kind. Be constructive. Assume good intent.**

Debate ideas, not people. Help others learn. Walk away from bad-faith arguments. If something feels off, tell the maintainer privately on GitHub — not in a public issue.

## Our standards

We welcome:

- Questions from newcomers (there are no “dumb” ones)
- Thoughtful feedback on code, docs, and design
- Bug reports and reproduction steps
- Patches and documentation improvements, large or small

Please avoid:

- Insults, sarcasm aimed at people, or pile-ons
- Harassment, discrimination, or sexualized language
- Sharing someone else’s private details without consent
- Sustained disruption of issues, PRs, or discussions

We are building reporting tools, not a courtroom — but we do take repeated or serious misconduct seriously.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces: GitHub issues and pull requests, release discussions, and anywhere you represent GenExPlus in public (talks, posts, etc.).

## Reporting (GitHub only)

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this code, **contact the maintainer privately on GitHub**:

**[@StefanBln](https://github.com/StefanBln)**

Please do **not** open a public issue for conduct matters. Use GitHub’s private contact options (message or profile contact, if available) or report specific comments via GitHub’s abuse reporting where appropriate.

You do not need a perfect account of events — a short description is enough to start. Reports are handled confidentially.

## What happens next

The maintainer will review reports fairly and proportionally. Depending on severity, responses may include:

1. **Conversation** — clarifying expectations in private  
2. **Warning** — formal notice; further incidents may lead to restrictions  
3. **Temporary restriction** — limited participation in project spaces for a period  
4. **Permanent ban** — for serious or repeated violations  

Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is not acceptable.
